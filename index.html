    Abstract

    Nowadays, text to speech (TTS) system has been able to generate high-fidelity human-like speech. However, it is difficult for the TTS system to maintain the quality of generated speech when there is little training data available for a specific speaker. Therefore, we propose the voice cloning system TSCT-TTS, aiming to clone the speaker identity of a target speaker using only a few utterances. Based on the advanced FastSpeech 2 TTS system, we modified its feed-forward Transformer block and used the latent speaker vector to explicitly control its hidden state. To achieve this, we fusion the local level feature coded by the Gated Convolutional Network (GCN) based module and the utterance level feature coded by the Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) based module. Experiment shows that our approach can custom the voice for a target speaker with 15 sentences. Besides, our proposed TSCT-TTS needs little computation resources by fine-tuning 5k steps on the pretrained multi-speaker based FastSpeech 2 model. Compared with baseline systems, our system demonstrates better all-around performance in the subjective and objective evaluation metrics.
